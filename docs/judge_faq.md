# Judge FAQ

## What is Analytics Workstation?

Analytics Workstation is an evidence-governed AI investigation platform. It helps users move from a business question to a traceable recommendation by preserving evidence, belief changes, claim verification, and decision readiness.

## Why not just use ChatGPT?

ChatGPT can summarize and reason, but it does not automatically preserve the deterministic evidence trail, governed action boundaries, replayable investigation state, artifact lineage, or decision-readiness checks. Analytics Workstation gives AI a structured operating environment instead of asking it to improvise from a prompt.

## Why not just use a BI dashboard?

Dashboards show state. They rarely preserve why an investigation moved from one explanation to another, what evidence weakened rejected explanations, or whether the final recommendation survived skeptical review.

## Why not just use AutoML?

AutoML optimizes model selection and performance. Analytics Workstation is about accountable investigation: why the analysis was run, what uncertainty remained, how evidence changed the recommendation, and whether the conclusion deserves action.

## What does GPT-5.6 do?

GPT-5.6 handles investigation framing, semantic synthesis, belief-evolution narrative, recommendation explanation, claim verification, and integrity-review language. Deterministic services compute the analytical facts.

## What does Codex demonstrate here?

Codex helped build the system through many incremental architecture, UI, QA, and documentation phases. The project demonstrates Codex as an engineering accelerator for a large, coherent software system, not merely as a code snippet generator.

## Is the AI allowed to run arbitrary actions?

No. AI actions are bounded by provider abstraction, action contracts, governance policies, preflight checks, approvals, and deterministic validation. The Build Week path is intentionally controlled.

## Why governed investigations?

Because high-value decisions require more than a confident answer. They require knowing what was investigated, what was rejected, what evidence changed the belief, what assumptions remain, and whether the conclusion is ready for action.

## Why belief revision?

A serious investigation should be allowed to change its mind. Belief revision makes that change explicit instead of hiding it in a final summary.

## Why claim verification?

A recommendation is only useful if the user can trace it back to evidence. Claim verification answers, "Why should I believe this?"

## Why the integrity review?

The integrity review makes the workstation its own skeptical reviewer. It searches for reasons the recommendation could be wrong before asking the user to trust it.

## Is this a Shiny app?

Shiny is the local reactive engine. The product is Analytics Workstation: an evidence-centered analytical operating environment built on top of Shiny.
